# CV and cover-letter standard for Germany (2026)

This is a practical synthesis for AI-assisted tailoring. The vacancy and verified candidate facts always take priority.

## CV

- Keep it clear, reverse chronological, and at most two A4 pages. Use the existing upstream LaTeX template, restrained typography, standard headings, and selectable text.
- Tailor the headline/profile, skills order, and evidence bullets to each vacancy. Do not merely append vacancy keywords.
- Put the most relevant proof high on page one. For each recent role use 3–5 concise bullets describing action, equipment/context, and result. Use numbers only when verified.
- Distinguish hands-on experience from labs, self-study, and developing knowledge. Never upgrade familiarity into professional experience.
- Use exact terms from the advert only when true; include both expanded terms and common abbreviations where useful. Avoid keyword stuffing, hidden text, skill bars, decorative charts, and information trapped only in icons.
- List month/year, employer, role, location, relevant education/certificates, languages with honest levels, and applicable technical skills. Explain material gaps honestly if needed.
- In Germany, a photo, nationality, family status, and similar personal details are optional; do not add them automatically. Include work-authorisation wording only when confirmed and phrased accurately.
- Export PDF, run text extraction, check reading order, links, dates, contact details, page count, and filename. Do not claim “ATS compatible” until this check passes.

## Cover letter

- Create a fresh letter for the vacancy; one page and normally about 250–350 words. If the portal does not request a letter, prepare a shorter message instead of forcing one.
- Use the named contact when verified, exact role/title, company, place/date, and a professional subject. Match the advert's language and level.
- Opening: name the role and give one specific, credible reason for this company/work—not generic enthusiasm.
- Middle: connect the two or three most important requirements to concrete CV evidence. Address a likely concern briefly and positively when useful, such as relocation, language development, or availability.
- Closing: state contribution/readiness, availability if relevant, and invite an interview. Keep the tone direct and natural.
- Do not repeat the whole CV, praise the company without evidence, fabricate company facts, imitate legal certainty, or overstate motivation. Avoid stock AI phrases and dense paragraphs.
- Read the final letter aloud, check grammar and every factual claim, and make sure the candidate could comfortably defend it in an interview.

## Model tailoring checklist

1. Extract responsibilities, must-haves, preferences, language, work pattern, location, and application instructions from the full advert.
2. Map each important claim to a verified CV/user fact. Remove unsupported claims.
3. Choose a coherent candidacy story: what the candidate can do now, why the transition makes sense, and how they will close one realistic gap quickly.
4. Produce the tailored source files and PDFs, then compare the rendered documents with the advert once more.
5. Show the user material edits and any statement needing confirmation before submission.

## Source basis

- [Bundesagentur für Arbeit: Lebenslauf](https://www.arbeitsagentur.de/bildung/bewerbung/lebenslauf) recommends a clear, tailored, reverse-chronological CV of one to two pages and 3–5 bullets per role; it also states that a photo is optional.
- [Bundesagentur für Arbeit: Anschreiben](https://www.arbeitsagentur.de/bildung/bewerbung/anschreiben) recommends researching the employer, connecting strengths to requirements, proofreading, and checking AI-generated wording for truth and personal fit.
- [Make it in Germany: application process](https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/working-in-germany/job/application) describes the usual German application package for international candidates.
- [Europass: create a good CV](https://europass.europa.eu/en/create-europass-cv) emphasizes tailoring, clarity, reverse chronology, and evidence relevant to the role.

Treat “2026” as the review date, not a promise that hiring practice is universal. Follow the employer's stated instructions first.
